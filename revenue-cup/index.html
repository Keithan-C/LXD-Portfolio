The Revenue Cup

Performance-centred innovation challenge · Keithan Cokbilir

Ideas don't stay invisible because people lack creativity. They stay invisible because there's no structured pathway from idea to incubation — and no reason to believe that trying leads anywhere.

The Revenue Cup is not a training programme. It's an environment redesign. Four weeks, a live scoreboard, peer voting, a 6-week incubation arc, and baseline measurement built in from day one — designed to surface revenue ideas from teams who already have them but no channel to share them.

Design note
This is a pilot programme — designed in full, not yet run. The artefacts below show the complete design: what participants experience, how measurement works, and what happens to the winning idea across the incubation arc. The case study framework is built and ready. What comes next is a real team, real data, and a real outcome.

03

4-week challenge structure

Each week builds on the last. Power BI arrives in week 3 — after ideas are formed and motivation is high, not before. The work anchor nomination happens in week 1 before any idea is required. Week 2 is the highest-risk dropout moment — the mid-point nudge is a designed intervention, not an afterthought.

05

LXD completion pack — measurement, incubation & manager brief

Three LXD gaps addressed
Baseline questions before week 1 and repeated after week 4. A 6-week incubation arc from pitch day to leadership investment decision. A manager brief with three touchpoints, specific language for each, and designed responses to the four most common team objections.
The artefacts most likely to determine whether the programme actually changes behaviour — not what participants experience during four weeks, but what was measured before it started, what happens to the winning idea afterwards, and whether managers created the conditions for participation to be real.

If they don't use it — I've failed.

The Revenue Cup is not designed to produce completion rates or participation numbers. It is designed to produce one data-backed idea that reaches a leadership investment decision — and a team that knows how to surface commercial intelligence in a way they didn't before. If those two things don't happen, the design goes back to the action map.
