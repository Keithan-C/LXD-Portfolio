The gap is never knowledge alone. It's always environment, confidence, and a reason to act.

Most data literacy programmes miss this. They deliver content and measure completion. What they don't do is design for the moment someone sits in their first governance meeting, or opens Power BI with a real business question, or has to make a decision that used to go to IT. That's the moment that matters. That's what this programme is designed for.

Data literacy is where I see the capability-without-structure problem at its most consequential. And VeriTech Group is where I want to solve it — because an organisation built on rigour and standards gives a learning programme credibility before the first session runs.

01

Role-based learning journeys

Three interconnected learning journeys — one per role — each built around what people need to do on day one, not what they need to know.
Open learning journeys
02

Cross-role simulation

The centrepiece of session 4 — a realistic scenario where all three roles must reach a decision together, with role-specific briefs, designed information asymmetry, and a facilitator guide included.
Open simulation scenario
03

Accountability card sort

The 8-minute pre-session activity that replaces a cold start — learners assign 12 real scenarios to a role before arriving, so session 1 begins with genuine disagreement rather than a blank room.
Open card sort
04

Measurement, feedback and contingency

How we know the programme worked — a complete measurement arc from day zero to day 30, the mechanism that closes the Voice your blocks feedback loop, and a designed response for when participation falls below threshold.
Open measurement framework

If they don't use it — I've failed.

This programme is not designed to produce completion rates. It is designed to produce Data Owners who make decisions, Data Stewards who resolve conflicts, and Use Case Owners who submit briefs that technical teams can act on. If those behaviours don't change at day 30, the design goes back to the action map.
