The Intelligence Sprint

Engineering intelligence programme · Keithan Cokbilir · LXD Advisory

ADAS and SDV engineering teams sit on technically and commercially valuable intelligence — about sensor performance, platform architecture trade-offs, supplier capability gaps, and regulatory risk. Right now that intelligence reaches product management through formal reviews that are too slow, too filtered, and too late to drive real decisions.

The Intelligence Sprint is not a training programme. It is a structured 4-week pilot that surfaces those insights and creates a documented pathway from engineering observation to product management action — with baseline measurement built in from day one and a 6-week development arc for the winning insight.

Design note
This is a pilot programme — designed in full, not yet run. The artefacts below show the complete design: the insight brief template participants complete in week 2, how measurement works, and what happens to the winning insight afterwards. The case study framework is built and ready. What comes next is a real engineering team, real data, and a real outcome.

01

Insight brief template

The most important single artefact in the Intelligence Sprint. A five-section structured template that engineers complete in week 2 — anchoring their insight to a live programme, describing the technical observation, translating it into a commercial or regulatory implication, and supporting it with real data. Without this template, week 2 produces inconsistent submissions that product management cannot evaluate.

02

4-week sprint structure

Week 1 anchors each insight to a live engineering programme. Week 2 is the submission and peer review moment. Week 3 releases a one-page engineering data job aid — three views that translate technical metrics into a commercial argument — at the moment when teams have a real question to answer. Week 4 is the review day with product management.
Open sprint brief →
03

Baseline measurement — engineering version

Three questions specific to an engineering audience. Question 1 measures commercial translation confidence — the primary skill gap. Question 2 measures belief that insights reach product management — the environment gap. Question 3 is the most powerful: "when was the last time a technical observation led to a commercial decision?" Most engineers will answer "I can't think of one." That answer is your case study's most compelling opening line.

"The intelligence already exists inside your engineering teams. The Sprint builds the pathway to surface it."

The Intelligence Sprint is not designed to teach engineers about commercial thinking. It is designed to surface what they already know and create a structured pathway for the best insights to reach product management with a brief they can act on. If no insights enter the development pathway, the design goes back to the action map.
