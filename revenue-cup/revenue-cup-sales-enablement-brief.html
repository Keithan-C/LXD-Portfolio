The problem
Your sales team is talking to customers every day. They know which objections are hardest to handle, which product gaps are losing deals, and which market signals your product team hasn't seen yet. Right now that intelligence lives in call notes, Slack threads, and individual reps' heads — and it never reaches the people who could act on it.
Where commercial intelligence gets lost in sales organisations
📉
Deal intelligence stays in the deal
Win/loss patterns, pricing objections, and competitive intelligence from individual deals never surface as patterns that revenue or product leadership can act on systematically.
🔇
Reps with insight but no channel
Experienced reps know what's working and what isn't. But there's no structured way to surface that knowledge — it surfaces in 1:1s with managers, not in a form that scales.
📊
CRM data used for reporting not insight
CRM and sales analytics tools are used to report on pipeline, not to surface the commercial patterns inside it. The data exists. The question is never asked of it.
How the Revenue Cup works — 4 weeks
Week 1
Anchor to a live deal or account
Each rep or team anchors their insight to a real account, deal, or market situation they're actively working. No hypotheticals — real commercial context only.
Week 2
Insight brief submitted
Teams submit a structured commercial insight — what they're seeing, why it matters, and what leadership could do with it. A live scoreboard goes live. Peer review opens.
Week 3
Data strengthens the brief
A one-page CRM and sales analytics job aid releases — three views that turn pipeline data into a commercial argument. Released when reps have a real question to answer.
Design decision
Week 4
Revenue review
Top 3 insights presented to revenue leadership. Winning insight enters a structured development pathway — handed to product, marketing, or sales leadership with a brief attached.
